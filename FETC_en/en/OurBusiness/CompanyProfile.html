Company Profile

Far Eastern Electronic Tolling Company (FETC) is one of over 200 affiliated companies of Far Eastern Group (FEG), which is a major conglomerate in Taiwan with business ranging in ten various industries. FEG aims to make every enterprise under its flag a leading company in the respective industry and has always taken "trustworthiness" as the guiding principle for business management and enshrined "Sincerity, Diligence, Thrift, Prudence, and Innovation" as its founding motto.FEG has successfully
developed and managed various businesses in communication the internet, technology innovation and so on.

FETC was founded in 2004 and was entrusted by Taiwan National Freeway Bureau (TANFB) to build and operate the Electronic Toll Collection (ETC) system for the nationwide freeways. In early 2006, FETC successfully launched entry-based ETC lanes to 23 toll stations. Later on, after eTag, which uses RFID as ETC technology, was introduced to the public in 2012, the utilization rate skyrocketed and has reached the threshold for TANB and FETC to introduce distance-based and multi-lane free flow (MLFF) tolling system with 319 gantries to the road users. The new tolling system was launched on December 30th 2013.

FETC is dedicated to Taiwan's freeway ETC total solution, including front-end and back-end systems as well as business model, such as planning, designing, construction, testing, and operation. FETC has achieved great success in implementing a multi-lane free flow, distance-based charging system since 2014. With utilization rate of over 94%, all vehicle users in Taiwan now enjoy driving on freeways without having to stop to pay tolls. Currently, more than 7 million vehicle users benefit from this ETC system with more than 4.8 billion accumulated transactions in the first year. Taiwan ETC system not only advances the intelligent transportation environment and better serves road users but also elevates Taiwan to the new era of intelligent transportation industry.

In order to implement the advanced national transportation policies, Far Eastern Electronic Toll Collection Co. (FETC) was commissioned by Freeway Bureau, MOTC to set up the Electronic Toll Collection (ETC) service. Major shareholders of FETC include Far Eastone Telecommunication Co., Ltd. (FET), TECO Electric & Machinery Co., Ltd. (TECO), Systex Corporation, and MiTAC Inc. FETC not only adopts the superior experiences from the four, but also incorporates the excellent operational strategies from the world and adopts the most advanced technology and at the same time embraces the following fundamental principles: be contingent, be truthful, and be innovative.

The per-entry-based construction of ETC was completed by 2005, and the operation started on February 10th, 2006. With distance-based multi-lane free flow ETC introduced on 30 December 2013, Taiwan's freeway transportation entered a new era. The traffic time from north to south of Taiwan is found to be shortened by average 10 minutes, with the highest reaches 40 minutes Besides, the reduction of oil consumption and less CO2 emission are also part of the benefits of ETC.. On top of these, ETC system can offer all vehicle users value-added services as such travel information, roadway information, mobile commerce, multimedia entertainments, and motorcade management.
